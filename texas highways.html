Texas Highways: Mobile App Concept

Collaboration with the Texas Highways (part of Texas Department of Transportation)

Problem space:
Texas Highways has done an exceptional job of driving tourism in Texas. However, their target audience have been Texans over 40 years old. They want to reach out to younger audiences by introducing a mobile app, as 94% of the people between age 18 to 49 owns a smartphone (Pew Research Center, Jun2019).

My Role: UX Designer

Duration: 4 months



From client kickoff, I wanted to learn the objective of the mobile app concept.


We came up with personas and scenarios to identify the app's opportunities. In addition, this process also help us identify the competitors of Texas Highways.





From personas and scenarios, I concluded that the focus should be on the explore and planning phase, since other competitors are doing well in other segments. Furthermore, Texas Highways puts emphasis on photographs, that could be the selling point of the app.


My partner and I then came up with a site map to conceptualize the overall functions of the app. Since Texas Highways emphasized on their content and photographs, we decided that the articles would be the main feature.

Next, I created our low fidelity prototype based on the site map and generated 3 scenarios to tested it out.



The medium fidelity prototype was built based on the user's feedback, including information architecture, wording, navigation, use of icon, etc.



Our final high fidelity prototype was refined based past iterations and their testing results.
